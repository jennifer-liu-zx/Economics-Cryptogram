"Everything is worth what its purchaser will pay for it."

- Publilius Syrus, 1st century BC

Publilius Syrus


This quote originates from Publilius Syrus’s collection of aphorisms, Sententiae (also known as The Moral Sayings of Publilius Syrus), published in 1855. Publilius Syrus was a Latin mime writer and a freed slave, whose sayings have been widely cited in antiquity and later periods for their wisdom and moral guidance. This particular maxim reflects the nature of value, suggesting that worth is determined subjectively rather than objectively, based on individual perception and willingness to pay.


While it applies to market dynamics and pricing, critics argue that it neglects intrinsic values such as ethical, cultural, or emotional worth and may perpetuate materialism or justify exploitative practices. Thus, it becomes not only a profound economic insight but also a point of ethical debate.
